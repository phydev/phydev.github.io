I currently work as a Data Scientist at the Norwegian Tax Administration (Skatteetaten), where I support the agency's data-driven vision by developing machine learning solutions for various applications.

Previously, I worked as a postdoc and researcher in Biostatistics at the University of Oslo (Norway). I developed clinical prognostic models to predict quality of life and survival in head and neck cancer patients. During this time, I also enhanced my data engineering skills by harmonizing and integrating datasets from different sources, using web scraping to acquire data, and developing REST APIs and dashboards for model deployment.

My education in Computational Physics provided me with strong analytical and mathematical skills, along with extensive experience in computational science. As a research assistant at the University of Coimbra (Portugal), I developed complex mechanistic models to simulate biological systems such as blood vessel growth, cell migration, and cancer metastasis.

Explore my website to learn more about my projects, publications, and the innovative work I continue to pursue at the intersection of data science and applied research.
