Big data and artificial intelligence for early detection and personalized treatment in head and neck cancer

Head and Neck Cancer (HNC) involves aggressive and heterogeneous tumors with a high fatality rate. Treatment may be extremely invasive and its impact on quality of life (QoL) can be devastating. In the last decade, an impressive amount of knowledge has been achieved in epidemiology and biology based on omics technologies. However, this wealth of data is not fully exploited, and its prognostic and predictive capabilities are far from optimally utilized.

My research project at the Oslo Center for Biostatistics and Epidemiology (OCBE) is integrated within the SuPerTreat and BD4QoL projects (see details below). Some of the main goals are:  

  • Exploit omics data, as an alternative or in addition to a currently used staging system to predict various outcomes of HNC patients.
  • Produce and validate actionable prognostic and predictive models and algorithms to orient personalized treatment decisions, and work towards the integration of these into clinical decision support tools.
  • Exploit behavioural measurements made by an app carried by HNC survivors, to determine current levels of QoL and to predict deteriorations or other anomalies as rapidly as possible.

 

SuPerTreat 

Supporting Personalized Treatment Decisions in Head and Neck Cancer through Big Data

"This project will focus on: validation of multifactorial methods combining existing clinically annotated omics datasets; investigation of ethical and legal aspects of data-driven clinical decision making vs. current evidence-based approach. We start from one of the world largest pool of treated HNC patients (approximately 2500), where the efficacy of the treatment has been recorded in varying forms together with a rich pool of omics and clinical data. In a 3 years study we will analyze these multi-source data using various types of classification, regression and statistical learning methods."  (Extract from the project abstract)

BD4QoL

Big Data Models and Intelligent tools for Quality of Life monitoring and participatory

"The EU-funded BD4QoL project aims to improve HNC survivors’ quality of life by developing a person-centred monitoring and follow-up plan. It will use artificial intelligence and Big Data collected from mobile devices, in combination with multi-source clinical and socioeconomic data and patients’ reported outcomes. Analysis of the quality of life indicators collected over time will facilitate early detection of risks, prevent long-term effects of treatment, and inform patients and caregivers for personalised interventions." (Extract from the project abstract)
